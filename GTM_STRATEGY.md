# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy: Credex AI Spend Audit

This document outlines a hyper-focused, zero-budget growth strategy to acquire the first 100 to 1,000 users for the AI Spend Audit SaaS, leveraging our built-in viral loops and community-led growth.

## 🎯 Exact Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

To grow efficiently, we must ignore enterprise giants initially and laser-focus on:
- **Title:** Founders, CTOs, and Head of Operations.
- **Company Size:** Seed to Series B startups (10 to 150 employees).
- **Pain Point:** They recently bought ChatGPT Team/Enterprise or Github Copilot for their entire organization without realizing that half the company barely uses it, or that they are on the wrong pricing tier. They are hyper-conscious of their SaaS burn rate.
- **Where they live:** Tech Twitter/X, YC Hacker News, r/SaaS, r/Entrepreneur, specialized Slack communities.

## 🧲 Core Acquisition Channels

1. **Engineering-as-Marketing (The Product Itself):** The Audit tool *is* the marketing. We aren't selling a PDF; we are providing instant, personalized financial value for free in exchange for an email.
2. **Founder-Led Social:** Building in public on Twitter/X and LinkedIn.
3. **Niche Communities:** Subreddits, IndieHackers, and Discord/Slack groups for bootstrapped founders.

## 🚀 The Product Hunt Strategy

Product Hunt is a launchpad, not a sustained growth channel. 
- **The Hook:** *"We built a free calculator that exposes how much you are overpaying for AI."* 
- **Preparation (T-Minus 14 Days):** 
  - Build a hype list by teasing the dynamic OG images on Twitter: *"I just ran my company through our own tool and found out we are wasting $4k/yr on ChatGPT seats we don't need."*
- **Launch Day Assets:** 
  - **Maker Comment:** Tell the story of *why* you built this (e.g., "I got annoyed that my startup was paying $600/mo for AI tools we barely used").
  - **Video/GIF:** A fast-paced, 15-second GIF showing the form submission, the animated counter rolling up to "$5,000 Saved," and the Gemini AI summary generating.
- **The Ask:** Do not ask for upvotes. Ask people to "Drop your audit link in the comments so we can see who saved the most!" (This drives engagement and keeps the post trending).

## 🐦 Twitter/X Strategy

- **The "Audit Tear-Down" Format:** Find public SaaS founders and tweet at them. *"Hey @Founder, I noticed you have 40 engineers. I ran a hypothetical AI spend audit for your team—if you switch from Copilot to Cursor Pro, you'd save $X/yr and get better context. Here is the link."*
- **Flex the OG Images:** The dynamic share images were built specifically for Twitter. Regularly post screenshots of massive savings results. 
- **The Giveaway Loop:** *"Reply with your tech stack below, and I'll generate a custom AI spend audit link for your startup and DM it to you."* (This manipulates the Twitter algorithm by driving massive reply velocity).

## 👾 Reddit Strategy

Reddit aggressively bans self-promotion. We must use the "Trojan Horse of Value" approach.
- **Target Subreddits:** `r/SaaS`, `r/Entrepreneur`, `r/startups`, `r/SideProject`.
- **The Post Format:** DO NOT link the product directly in the post. 
  - **Title:** *"We audited 50 startups. 80% of them are wasting at least $2k/year on ChatGPT Team."*
  - **Body:** Write a massive, highly valuable text post breaking down the exact pricing math between ChatGPT Plus vs Team, Cursor vs Copilot, and Claude Pro. Give away the secrets.
  - **The Hook:** At the very bottom: *"P.S. I got tired of doing this math manually, so I built a free tool that calculates this for you based on your team size. Let me know if you want the link."* 
  - **Result:** People will DM you or comment asking for the link, bypassing the self-promo filters.

## 💸 Zero-Budget Growth Tactics

- **Cold Emailing (Micro-Targeted):** Use Apollo or built-in LinkedIn search to find "Head of Ops" at startups that recently raised a Seed round. 
  - *Subject:* Your AI software spend
  - *Body:* "Hey [Name], I noticed [Company] just crossed 30 employees. Most teams your size are overpaying for AI licenses. I built a free tool to check if you are. Take the 30-second audit here: [Link]."
- **Partner with Fractional CFOs:** Reach out to fractional CFOs on LinkedIn. Offer them the tool to use for *their* clients as a value-add.

## 💯 The First 100 Users Plan (Days 1-7)

1. **Day 1-2 (The Inner Circle):** Force your friends, previous coworkers, and mastermind groups to run an audit. Get the first 15-20 users. Ask them to click the "Share on X" button.
2. **Day 3 (The Reddit Drop):** Execute the "Trojan Horse" post on `r/SaaS`. Goal: 30-40 users.
3. **Day 4-5 (Twitter Hustle):** Execute the "Reply with your stack" Twitter thread. Goal: 20-30 users.
4. **Day 6-7 (Cold Outreach):** Send 50 highly personalized cold emails or LinkedIn DMs to target ICPs. Goal: 10-20 users.

## 🦠 Amplifying The Viral Loop

The architecture of this app was specifically designed to be a viral growth loop. Here is how we maximize it:
- **Ego-Baiting:** The core emotion we are targeting is *pride*. Founders want to show off how lean and optimized they are. 
- **The "Share on X" CTA:** Currently, the button pre-fills a tweet saying "I just saved $X...". We can A/B test the copy to be more competitive: *"My startup's AI stack is 100% optimized. We are saving $X/yr. Check your stack here: [Link]"*
- **The Watermark:** The OpenGraph images dynamically render "Powered by Credex". Every time a user proudly shares their savings in an investor update or a Slack channel, they are giving us free billboard space. 
- **Slack/Discord Unfurling:** Because we explicitly fixed the Node.js OpenGraph image rendering, dropping the URL into any tech Slack channel will instantly render a massive, colorful image displaying a dollar amount. This acts as visual clickbait in busy chat rooms, driving coworkers to click and run their own audits.
