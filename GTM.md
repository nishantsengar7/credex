# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

## 🎯 Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
Our primary target user is the **Head of Operations** or **VP of Finance** at **Series A tech startups (30 to 80 employees)**. 

At this stage, companies have recently scaled their engineering and marketing teams and distributed corporate cards, leading to a sudden surge in unmanaged SaaS subscriptions. These decision-makers are tasked by the CEO or Board to reduce operational burn but lack the time to manually audit which developers are using Cursor versus GitHub Copilot, or which copywriters are paying for duplicate ChatGPT Team seats.

---

## 🔍 Pre-Audit Intent Signals
Before seeking a tool like ours, our ICP is performing these actions:
* **Googling:** `"how to audit corporate card SaaS subscriptions"`, `"reduce startup SaaS burn rate"`, `"Cursor Pro vs GitHub Copilot enterprise pricing"`, or `"duplicate AI tool licenses in Slack"`.
* **Scrolling:** Reading Twitter/X threads on *"lean operations for Series A startups"* or browsing Hacker News posts like *"Ask HN: How do you manage team AI software subscriptions?"*

---

## 📍 Online Hangouts
We will locate and engage our ICP in the following niches:
* **Reddit:** `r/CFO`, `r/SaaS`, and `r/startups`.
* **Private Slack/Discord Communities:** The "Lattice Humans" Slack community, "Finance Ops" Discord, and private YC founder channels.
* **X (Twitter):** Curated lists of startup CFOs and operations leaders.

## 🚀 Acquiring the First 100 Users (30-Day Plan, $0 Budget)
We will execute three specific, zero-budget tactics:
1. **The Custom CSV Audit (Days 1–10):** Scrape LinkedIn to identify 50 SF/NYC tech startups with 30–80 employees. Audit their public engineering team size. Send a highly personalized cold LinkedIn message directly to the Head of Operations: *"Hey [Name], I noticed your engineering team is using both Cursor and Copilot. Most Series A teams waste $3k/yr here. I ran your team size through a calculator: here is a custom link [Link] showing how you can save $4,200/yr."*
2. **The Reddit Trojan Horse (Days 11–20):** Post a detailed, analytical breakdown on `r/SaaS` comparing Cursor Business vs Pro seat optimization math. Give away all the pricing secrets. At the very bottom, add: *"P.S. I got tired of calculating this manually for our portfolio companies, so I built a free audit page. DM me if you want the link."* This bypasses self-promo bans and drives direct inbox requests.
3. **Fractional CFO Partnerships (Days 21–30):** DM 25 fractional CFOs on LinkedIn (who manage financial operations for 5–10 startups each). Provide them with the audit tool as a free value-add they can run for their clients to instantly demonstrate cost savings and build trust.

## ⚡ The Unfair Distribution Channel
Because Credex is a corporate spend management card platform, we have direct visibility into real-time transaction feeds. Our unfair advantage is the ability to automatically identify transaction spikes for merchants like OpenAI, Anthropic, or Cursor. We can target cardholders directly in their spend dashboard with a single click: *"You spent $1,200 on OpenAI last month. Click here to run our free Audit Engine and check for duplicate seats."*

If this GTM strategy is successful, our Week-1 metrics will be:

* **5 unique shared results** posted on X or LinkedIn by startup founders (tracked via our Supabase public share metrics).
* **1 partnership** established with a fractional CFO, driving 20+ automated audits from their clients.
